# Music Review Content Citation & Copyright Compliance Scheme

## 1. Copyright Risk Assessment & Compliance Framework

### 1.1 Legal Basis (US & China)
*   **United States (Fair Use)**: Section 107 of the Copyright Act allows for "fair use" for purposes such as criticism, comment, and news reporting. Key factors include:
    *   **Transformative Use**: Adding new meaning or expression (e.g., aggregating scores for comparison).
    *   **Amount Used**: Using small excerpts rather than full text is safer.
    *   **Market Effect**: The use must not substitute for the original work (i.e., it shouldn't stop people from visiting the original site).
*   **China (Limitations on Rights)**: Article 24 of the PRC Copyright Law permits "appropriate quotation" from published works for the purpose of introducing or commenting on a work, provided the source and author are named.

### 1.2 Risk Levels
*   **Low Risk**: Displaying numerical scores (facts), album metadata, and short snippets (1-2 sentences) with direct links.
*   **Medium Risk**: Displaying paragraph-long summaries generated by AI without clear attribution.
*   **High Risk**: Republishing full reviews, using high-res editorial images without license, or scraping content against Terms of Service (ToS).

## 2. Citation Norms & Standards

### 2.1 Acceptable Citation Ratios
*   **Snippet Length**: Maximum **50-80 words** or **10%** of the original text, whichever is shorter.
*   **Formatting**:
    *   Use quotation marks or italics for direct quotes.
    *   Clearly distinguish between "Summary" (AI-generated or editorial) and "Direct Quote".

### 2.2 Attribution Specification
Every review display must include:
1.  **Source Name** (e.g., "Pitchfork", "Rolling Stone").
2.  **Author Name** (if available).
3.  **Functional Link**: "Read Full Review" pointing to the original URL.
4.  **Disclaimer**: "Content excerpted for educational/informational purposes."

## 3. Authorization & Cooperation Workflow

### 3.1 Step-by-Step Process
1.  **Identify High-Value Sources**: List top 10-20 review sites (e.g., Metacritic, Pitchfork, AllMusic).
2.  **Check Terms of Service**: Look for API availability or "permissible use" clauses.
3.  **Outreach**:
    *   Send a standard "Content Usage Request" email.
    *   *Template*: "We are building a personal music library tool and would like to display snippets of your reviews with direct back-links to drive traffic to your site..."
4.  **Partnership**: Offer data exchange or traffic metrics in return for permission.

## 4. Fallback Strategies (When Authorization Fails)

### 4.1 Strategy A: "The Snippet + Link" (Default)
*   Display only the numeric score (fact, not copyrightable) and a one-sentence summary written by your own editors or AI (transformative).
*   Provide a "Read on [Source]" button.

### 4.2 Strategy B: User-Generated Content (UGC)
*   Encourage users to write their own reviews.
*   "Be the first to review this album!"

### 4.3 Strategy C: Editorial Original
*   Hire freelance music writers to produce original 100-word capsule reviews for top albums.

## 5. Technical Implementation: Automated Attribution

### 5.1 Data Structure (Supabase)
Ensure the `reviews_aggregator` table supports attribution fields:
```sql
CREATE TABLE reviews_aggregator (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT uuid_generate_v4(),
  album_id UUID REFERENCES albums(id),
  source_name TEXT NOT NULL,      -- e.g., "Pitchfork"
  source_url TEXT NOT NULL,       -- Full URL to original review
  author_name TEXT,               -- e.g., "Ryan Dombal"
  score NUMERIC(3,1),             -- e.g., 8.5
  snippet TEXT,                   -- Short excerpt (<100 words)
  is_official_license BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE
);
```

### 5.2 Frontend Component (ReviewModal.js)
*   **Mandatory Fields**: The UI must refuse to render the review if `source_url` is missing.
*   **No-Follow Links**: All external links should have `rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"` to avoid SEO penalties or implying endorsement.

## 6. Periodic Review Mechanism

### 6.1 Monthly Compliance Audit
*   **Checklist**:
    *   [ ] Are all links functional? (Dead links reduce "fair use" defense).
    *   [ ] Have any sources updated their ToS to explicitly ban scraping/snippets?
    *   [ ] Are snippet lengths within the 50-word limit?
*   **Takedown Procedure**: If a DMCA notice or complaint is received, immediately hide the content via a database flag (`is_visible = false`) and notify the user.
